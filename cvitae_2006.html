Curriculum vitae et studiorum of

Silvano Martello

Didactic Activity

Silvano Martello, born in Bologna on April 20, 1948, took his degree in Electronic Engineering in 1973 at the University of Bologna.

On November 1, 1974, he was nominated holder of a teaching and scientific formation study grant from the Ministry of Education and carried out activity in this capacity until March 6, 1980. During that period he ran the practical courses in Logic Networks and Computers and in Electronic Computation in the Faculty of Engineering of the University of Bologna.

From March 7, 1980, to July 31, 1980, he was external Lecturer of Numerical Analysis and Programming at the Faculty of Engineering of Bologna University.

On August 1, 1980, he was incorporated in the Confirmed Researcher category of the Engineering Faculty of the University of Bologna and acted as Researcher and internal Lecturer of Numerical Analysis and Programming until July 28, 1985.

From July 29, 1985, to October 31, 1990, he was Associate Professor of Operations Research and in charge of teaching the same at the Faculty of Engineering of the University of Bologna.

From November 1, 1990, to October 31, 1994, he was Full Professor of Operations Research and Management Science at the Faculty of Mathematical, Physical and Natural Sciences of the University of Turin. Moreover he was also in charge of the Operations Research course in the Faculty of Engineering of the University of Bologna.

From November 1, 1994, he has been Full Professor of Operations Research at the Faculty of Engineering of the University of Bologna.

Scientific Activity

Silvano Martello has been mainly interested in problems of Numerical Simulation and Combinatorial Optimization.

In the first sector he performed comparative analyses of the most widely used simulation languages.

As regards Combinatorial Optimization, to which he has dedicated most of his research commitment, he has occupied himself mainly with algorithms, both exact and approximate, for the solution of Graph Theory problems and Integer Linear Programming problems. Within the compass of these researches, aiming at defining and evaluating (both theoretically and experimentally) new algorithms, he has thoroughly examined the following problems:

(a) Hamiltonian circuits;

(b) Travelling salesman;

(c) Minimum equivalent graph;

(d) Knapsack:

  • single knapsack;
  • multiple knapsack;
  • surveys;

(e) Change making;

(f) Subset-sum;

(g) Assignment;

(h) Balanced optimization;

(i) Scheduling;

(j) Partitioning;

(k) Bin-packing;

(l) branch-and-bound techniques;

(m) bibliographies and general subjects.

He has published seven volumes of a didactic nature, a monograph of mathematical software and a volume of the Wiley-Interscience Series in Discrete Mathematics and Optimization; he also took care, as Editor, of a volume in the Annals of Discrete Mathematics North-Holland series, two special issues of INFOR, two special issues of Discrete Applied Mathematics, a special issue of European Journal of Operational Research, a volume of the Wiley-Interscience Series in Discrete Mathematics and Optimization and a volume of Kluwer Academic Publishers.

He has carried out research for a bilateral Italy-UK project, in the C.N.R. Project on Computer Science, the C.N.R. Project on Transportation and various Ministry of University national projects, both as researcher and in charge of operative units. From 1986 to 1993 he has also been Researcher at the Centre for Operator-Computer Interaction (C.I.O.C.) - C.N.R., Bologna. He has been in charge for a bilateral Italy-Hungary project.

Intensive seminar activity in important national, European and extra-European universities, as well as holding lessons at international seminars.

He has taken part in numerous national and international congresses, often as an invited speaker. He has organized and chaired numerous congress sessions, and has been a member of organizing committees for national and international congresses.

In his own scientific field he regularly acts as referee for the principal national and international journals.

He is Associate Editor of the journals INFOR and Investigacion Operativa, Advisory Editor of the Journal of Heuristics, co-Editor of the Mathematical Software Section of the journal Discrete Applied Mathematics and Editor-in-chief of the journals Ricerca Operativa and 4OR. He is in the board of Editors of the series SIAM Monographs on Discrete Mathematics and Applications.

He is Coordinator of the ``European Chapter in Combinatorial Optimization". He is Senior Fellow of the Center for Management of Operations and Logistics of the University of Texas at Austin.
